Healthcare apps in 2022

It is clear that people have become more conscious about their health. And with the proliferation of technology, access to online healthcare consultation has also increased. According to a report published by Mckinsey, doctors render online consultation to patients 50 to 175 times higher than the pre-pandemic levels. These stats indicate the importance of app development for healthcare.

Healthcare apps, especially after the pandemic has seen a humongous rise and investments from several businesses. Also, the advent of new-age technologies such as Artificial (AR) and Augmented Reality (AR) have aided in the transformation of the healthcare sector and helped the patients dramatically.

Several health app features allow the patients to have consultations with the doctor without even stepping out of their homes. And the horizon of the healthcare industry is not limited to just telehealth. By using AI and Machine Learning (ML), different types of healthcare apps can follow human emotions and provide them with better treatment.

Therefore, in this blog, we will study in detail the different health app features that are needed to make a competent healthcare app in 2022.

Why is healthcare tech important in the modern era?
Before getting into the details of different types of healthcare apps and the type of healthcare software required, it is necessary to understand the importance of healthcare tech.

The innovation in the realm of healthcare tech has reached a whole new level. If we consider their importance in the US economy, it will account for 26% of the GDP by 2040. Now, these numbers are staggering! Even the use of telemedicine has increased in the US after the pandemic and has reached a whopping 46% by the end of 2021.

These numbers are mind-boggling, but they state the importance of healthcare tech in the coming time clearly. This niche has a large appetite for app development and will need good investment in the future. So, the business houses that ride on the bandwagon will enjoy the early-bird advantage in the future.
